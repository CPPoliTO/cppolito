SWERC

SWERC is the qualification contest for ICPC (International Collegiate Programming Contest) for the south-western Europe (with the addition of Israel), in which Italian Universities can partecpiate.

This year, for the first time, the competition has been organized by the Politecnico di Milano, after last year's online edition.

The contest is made up of usually 12 problems to solve in the minimum time possible, for each minute used to solve one problem a penalty point is added to your score. Penalty points are used in the case in which two (or more) teams solved the same number of problems (the less the better). This year, the first classified team (Raw Pots of Harbour.Space University) has solved ten of the twelve provided problems.

Our teams

Since, this year, the number of request of partecipation was greater than the maximum number of people we could enroll to the contest, we organized a "selection contest" open to everyone who was IPCP-elegible. After the selection, we made a ranking based on the results of the contests and then we created the groups of three that we registered for the competition.


Lastly, we would like to thank DAUIN to letting us partecipate, professor Giovanni Squillero to have been our connection point between us and the department and SWERC organization, to Politecnico di Milano and all the staff members and people who made the competition possible.
